Likes

These are the foods you have specifically told us you like. Don't worry, you don't have to tell us every food that you like, but we will base some of the recipes you see on these foods that you really like. In some cases they could be the star of the show and in others, the perfect accoumpanyment.

We will also show you recipes which include other ingredients, as long as you haven't said they are a dislike, allergen or not included within your diet.

Dislikes, Allergens and Diet

Dislikes are the foods that you have said that you do not like. As such the recipes that we recommend will not include these items. If we find a recipe that you might like which includes one of these items, we will provide a substitution or make you aware that it can be removed from the recipe.

If you have provided your allegen information or the type of diet you chose to follow, we will not include these ingredients in your recipes.

Wildcard

The wildcard is an ingredient which is not in your likes or dislikes. We think it is a great chance to introduce you to new ingredients which you may have not thought to include or maybe not come accross before. Make sure to update us on socials if you find a new found love for one of our Wildcard ingredients or Recipes!


Use the hashtag #fussyfoodiewildcard if you use one of our wildcard recipes

This weeks Wildcard is... Samphire

Samphire is a plant that grows in salt water and has a distrinctly crisp and salty flavour. Samphire is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Popular recipes this week

Lamb Tagine

Tantelize your tastebuds with this flavoursome Lamb Tagine. Not too hot, not too mild just vastly aromatic. Perfect if you have other things to do. This dish can be prepared in advanced and put in the oven to allow time to socialize! Dress with pomegranate seeds, fresh herbs, dried fruit and chopped nuts and serve with giant cous cous to take this dish from stew to colourful creation!

Vegan Masaman Curry

Add in any veg you would like for this Vegan Masaman Curry. Not meat doesn't mean no protein - with peanuts, chickpeas and peas. You can also switch things up by blending the curry and turning this into a Vegetable Masaman Soup. You favourite combination is switching the rice for adding sweet potato to the curry.

Chicken Pho

Great for the winter, this Chicken Pho is filled with fresh ingredients and lots of liquid. You can decide whether to make this the easy or hard way by either making or buying chciken bone broth. If you have leftover chicken from the Sunday Roast use it to make a broth or buy some from your local supermarket or butchers.

Mushroom and Spinach Lasagne

This creamy creation is a great use for spinach which is starting to whilt in the fridge. Make the dish healthier by using half the pasta and peeled courgette slices. We love to pair this recipe with a large green salad with a vinigarette to create a sharp contrast with the creamy cheesy goodness.

Lemon Drizzle Cake

An absolute crowd pleaser! This cake will win your work bake off and win round your in-laws. With the fresh citrus flavour, this cake is wonderfully soft and fluffy with just the right amount of sweet.

Salmon fishcakes

Most of us don't eat enough oily fish and as a great surce of omega 3 and 6, salmon is a great ingredient to use. If you want to do more than just baking fish fillets, try making these salmon fishcakes with dhill. You get to choose how fishy you want them to be!
